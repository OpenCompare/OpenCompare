This is a '''comparison of orbital launch systems'''. The following exposes the full list of [[Comparison of solid-fuelled orbital launch systems|conventional orbital launch system]]s. For the short simple list of conventional launcher families, see: [[Comparison of orbital launchers families]].

[[Spacecraft propulsion]] is any [[propulsion|method used to accelerate]] [[spacecraft]] and artificial [[satellite]]s. A conventional [[solid rocket]] or a conventional [[solid-fuel rocket]] is a rocket with a motor that uses [[solid propellant]]s ([[fuel]]/[[oxidizer]]). [[Orbit]]al [[launch vehicle|launch systems]] are rockets and [[non-rocket spacelaunch|other systems]] capable of placing [[payload]]s into or [[escape trajectory|beyond]] [[Earth orbit]]. All current spacecraft use conventional chemical rockets ([[bipropellant rocket|bipropellant]] or [[solid rocket|solid-fuel]]) for launch, though some have used [[air-breathing engine]]s on their [[Multistage rocket|first stage]].
